Teaching

I am passionate about teaching the social science statistics in all its forms, from basic probability and data management to ongoing methodological debates about causal inference. As an instructor at the graduate and undergraduate level, I have sought to instill quantiative literacy in students through hands-on experience doing research about topics that interest them.

SOCIOL 333: Quantiative Analysis of Sociological Data (2020)

Sole instructor

This course serves to introduce undergraduate sociology majors to quantitative approaches in the social science. Topics include probability and statistics, sampling and inference, linear and multiple regression, and writing about quantiative results. SOCIOL 722: Graduate Statistics I (Fall 2017, 2019, 2020)

Lab instructor

This course is the first in a two-semester sequence in statistical methods required for all first-year graduate students in the sociology department. The course covers probability and statistics, hypothesis testing, linear regression, multiple regression, maximum likelihood estimation, generalized linear models, model disagnostics, and model selection. As lab instructor, I led a two-and-a-half hour section that reinforced and extended the main topics of the course, instructed students in statistical software, and highlighted topics not covered in the main lectures.

SOCIOL 723: Graduate Statistics II (Spring 2017, 2018, 2020, 2021)

Lab instructor

This course is the second of a two-semester sequence for first-year graduate students. Topics included causal inference, matching and weighting, instrumental variables, panel data, multi-level models, and Bayesian approaches. As lab instructor, I led a two-and-a-half hour section that reinforced and extended the main topics of the course, instructed students in statistical software, and highlighted topics not covered in the main lectures.

SOCIOL 110D: Sociological Perspectives (Fall 2016)

Teaching assistant

This course serves as an introduction to sociological analysis and the variety of perspectives present in sociology. Topics included identity, race, deviance, social determinants of health, social networks, organizations, and social movements. Throughout the course, students designed and executed individual research projects. As a teaching assistant I led two weekly discussion sections, provided instruction in how to design and execute research, and provided regular feedback on student research papers.
